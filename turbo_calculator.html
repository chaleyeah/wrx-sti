Build Notes
Defaults set for stock EJ257 — 150 ci / 2,457 cc (99.5 mm × 79 mm). Bump displacement for overbore/stroker builds. The original spreadsheet used 151.07 ci, which implies ~99.85 mm bore — adjust if your build is actually overbored.
BSFC defaults to 0.63 to match the source spreadsheet. Garrett's published guideline for gas turbo is 0.55. Lower BSFC means a more efficient engine and a smaller required turbo. Keeping a higher BSFC builds in a safety margin for sizing.
VE 1.00 assumes a well-built motor with good cams and big plumbing. Stock long-block with stock cams runs closer to 0.92–0.95 at peak.
IAT 110 °F is post-intercooler. Air-to-air FMIC at full boost typically lands 100–130 °F; air-to-water can drop to 80–100 °F; stock/undersized cores climb past 140 °F. Hotter air = lower density = more airflow needed for the same mass.
ΔP (Pressure Drop) accounts for losses across the air filter, intercooler core, charge piping, and throttle body. 4.5 psi is reasonable for a well-built setup with a quality FMIC; restrictive setups can hit 6–8 psi.
Formulas: Airflow = (HP × AFR × BSFC) / 60 · MAP = (Airflow × R × (IAT + 460)) / (VE × RPM/2 × CID), R = 639.6 · Boost = MAP − Ambient · PR = (MAP + ΔP) / Ambient
