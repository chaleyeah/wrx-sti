Build Notes
Defaults set for stock EJ257 — 150 ci / 2,457 cc (99.5 mm × 79 mm). Bump displacement for overbore/stroker builds. The source spreadsheet used 151.07 ci, which implies ~99.85 mm bore — adjust if your build is actually overbored.
Fuel selection — Picking 93 or E85 from the dropdown loads typical AFR and BSFC defaults plus appropriate slider ranges. E85's stoichiometric AFR is 9.765 (vs 14.7 for gasoline) and BSFC runs about 35% higher because ethanol has lower energy density per pound. Counterintuitively, the air-flow per HP is actually slightly lower on E85 — ethanol carries oxygen in the fuel molecule, so the AFR × BSFC product comes out lower. In practice E85's bigger advantage is knock resistance, which lets you run more boost (and more HP) for the same compressor flow.
BSFC defaults to 0.63 on 93 to match the source spreadsheet. Garrett's published guideline for gas turbo is 0.55. Lower BSFC means a more efficient engine and a smaller required turbo. Keeping a higher BSFC builds in a safety margin for sizing.
VE 1.00 represents the engine's peak volumetric efficiency, used at peak operating conditions. A well-built motor with good cams and big plumbing hits 1.0; stock long-blocks with stock cams run closer to 0.92–0.95.
Engine Curve — Spool / Peak Torque / Peak HP define how boost ramps across the RPM band: zero below Spool, linear ramp from Spool to Peak Torque (where full boost is reached), held flat through Peak HP, then a small taper above (5%/1000 rpm). Set Peak HP equal to redline if you want no taper.
Compressor maps shown here are approximations derived from each manufacturer's published specifications (max flow, max PR, peak efficiency location, geometry). They're useful for relative comparison and to see roughly where the engine sits — but for authoritative numbers always cross-reference Garrett's, BorgWarner's, or Turbosmart's official map PDFs. Trajectory points (green) are computed directly from your inputs.
Reading the map — Operating points should fall inside or near the efficiency island during peak power. Points LEFT of the surge line risk compressor surge. Points RIGHT of the choke line mean you've exceeded the compressor's flow capacity (turbo too small for your power target).
Formulas: Airflow = (HP × AFR × BSFC) / 60 · MAP = (Airflow × R × (IAT + 460)) / (VE × RPM/2 × CID), R = 639.6 · Boost = MAP − Ambient · PR = (MAP + ΔP) / Ambient
